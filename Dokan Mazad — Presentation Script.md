## SLIDE 5: What Is Not Working

**Headline:** The presence does not match the product.

**On screen:**
No content that introduces Korean car imports to a buyer who has never considered it.
No process transparency. The buyer cannot see what happens between payment and delivery.
No consistent human face on camera. Trust is built by people, not car photos.
Content that looks AI-generated. In a fraud-aware market, that reads as a red flag.
No answer to the most important question buyers ask before committing: **مين جرب؟**

The brand's biggest advantage — direct auction access, end-to-end expertise — is invisible in everything that currently exists publicly.

## SLIDE 7: The Website and Domain Problem

**Headline:** 30 active versions of the brand. One buyer trying to figure out which one is real.

**On screen:**
SSL certificate records reveal 30 active operator subdomains under dokanmazad.com.

**Named:** alryan, abdullah, hussam, ahmed, osama, abumurad, mustafa, hussain, noor, noura, rayan, abufahad, abufahadalharbi, hallakorea, fkm, kafoocar, koreaauto, bcars, ezcars, ksa, numberone1

**Numeric:** 1, 100, 101, 102, 104, 105, 106, 107, 110

Each one resolves to a page that looks identical to the main site — except for the contact number.
Every one of them fragments the brand in Google's index.

Three of them — abufahad, hallakorea, ezcars — have expired SSL certificates.
Buyers following referral links hit a browser security warning before they see a single word.

There is also an independent domain — abufahadalharbi.com — built on the identical codebase, pulling from the same data feed, predating dokanmazad.com by ten months.
A buyer who encounters it during research has no way to understand the relationship.

## SLIDE 8: The Decision That Needs to Be Made

**Headline:** Two types of operator. One meeting to classify them.

**On screen:**
Dokan Mazad runs a B2B platform alongside the consumer service. This is not a problem — it is a second revenue stream. The only problem is execution.

**Type 1: Resellers**
Part of the Dokan Mazad sales network. They source leads and close deals.
Fix: move them off public subdomains. Replace with referral codes through dokanmazad.com. The buyer sees one brand. The reseller still gets credited.

**Type 2: Platform customers**
Independent businesses that purchased access to the data infrastructure.
Fix: they migrate to their own brand and their own domain. They are not Dokan Mazad.

Until this classification happens the brand cannot be consolidated and every riyal spent on content is partially undermined by 30 competing versions of the same page.

## SLIDE 9: The Solution
